📱 Phone alerts via ntfy.sh

Set this up once and your phone will buzz the instant a poll opens — even with your laptop muted or across the room. Takes about 3 minutes.

⚠️

Desktop notifications only work while your laptop screen is awake and Chrome is focused. Phone pushes reach you no matter what.

  1. 1
    Download the ntfy app on your phone

    ntfy is free and open-source — no account required. Pick your platform:

    📴 Android — Google Play 🍏 iPhone — App Store

    Android users: the F-Droid version is also available and delivers messages faster (uses no Google services).

  2. 2
    Pick a private topic name

    Your topic name is like a secret password — anyone who knows it can receive your notifications. Pick something random and personal. Good examples:

    jdoe-ut-polls-2026 longhorn42_alerts ecs347-myalerts
    Tip

    Mix your name initials with a random number. Avoid anything guessable like utpoll or your EID alone.

  3. 3
    Subscribe to your topic in the ntfy app

    Open the ntfy app you just installed and add a subscription:

    Android

    Tap the + button at the bottom-right → type your topic name → tap Subscribe.

    iPhone

    Tap + in the top-right corner → type your topic name → tap Subscribe.

    Leave the server as ntfy.sh (the default). Do not change it.

  4. 4
    Enable push alerts in the extension

    Click the UT Instapoll Alert icon in your Chrome toolbar (puzzle-piece menu if it's not pinned).

    1. Toggle Phone push (ntfy.sh) to ON.
    2. Type your topic name into the field exactly as you entered it in the app.
    3. Click Save.
    Tip

    To pin the extension: right-click the puzzle-piece 🧩 icon → Pin. The icon shows a live status dot while Instapoll is open.

  5. 5
    Send a test notification

    In the extension popup, click Send test push. Your phone should buzz within 1–3 seconds. If it does — you're done. ✅

    Phone didn't buzz? Scroll down to the FAQ for troubleshooting steps.

❓ Phone alerts FAQ

Is ntfy free? Do I need to pay or sign up?

ntfy is completely free for basic use. You do not need to create an account to use the public ntfy.sh server. Just install the app, subscribe to a topic, and you're ready. No email, no credit card, nothing.

Paid plans exist if you want reserved topic names (to prevent strangers from guessing your topic), but a sufficiently random topic name makes this unnecessary for most users.

What's a good topic name? How private is it?

On the public ntfy.sh server, topic names are not private by default — anyone who knows your exact topic name can subscribe to it and read your notifications. To stay safe:

  • Use at least 10–15 characters that mix letters and numbers.
  • Include something personal (initials, a number only you know).
  • Avoid dictionary words, your EID alone, course numbers alone, or anything obviously guessable.

Example of a bad name: utpoll. Example of a good name: jdoe-polls-4827.

My phone didn't buzz after the test. What's wrong?

Work through this checklist:

  1. Check the topic name matches exactly — it is case-sensitive. MyTopic ≠ mytopic.
  2. Open the ntfy app and confirm you see the subscription listed. If not, re-add it.
  3. Check Do Not Disturb / Focus mode on your phone — these can silently block notifications.
  4. Android only: Go to Settings → Apps → ntfy → Notifications and make sure notifications are allowed.
  5. iPhone only: Go to Settings → ntfy → Notifications and set Allow Notifications to ON.
  6. Try enabling Instant Delivery (Android only) — in the ntfy app, long-press your subscription → Enable instant delivery. This bypasses Firebase and is more reliable.
  7. Wait up to 30 seconds — the first message occasionally takes a moment if the app is freshly installed.
Will ntfy drain my phone battery?

Minimal impact. The standard mode uses Firebase Cloud Messaging (same infrastructure Gmail and every major app uses), which is battery-optimized by Android. The "Instant Delivery" mode runs a lightweight foreground service; it shows a persistent notification but has negligible power draw in practice.

The iOS app uses Apple Push Notification Service (APNs) — as battery-efficient as any other iOS app notification.

Can I receive alerts on multiple phones or devices?

Yes. Subscribe to the same topic name on every device you want to receive alerts on. All subscribed devices will receive the notification simultaneously. This is useful if you want both your phone and a tablet to buzz.

Are my Instapoll notifications stored anywhere?

Messages sent to a topic on ntfy.sh are cached briefly on ntfy's servers (typically for a few hours) in case your phone is offline. ntfy's privacy policy states they do not sell data or use it for advertising. The notifications contain only the poll alert text — no UT credentials or student data of any kind.

What if I change my topic name later?

Just update it in two places:

  1. In the ntfy app — add the new topic as a new subscription (you can remove the old one).
  2. In the extension popup — update the topic field and click Save.

There's no account to update — the topic name is the subscription.

Does this work if my phone is in another room or on silent?

Push notifications arrive regardless of distance. If your phone is on Silent mode, the notification will still appear in your notification shade (you'll feel a vibration if vibrate-on-silent is enabled). If your phone is in Do Not Disturb, notifications may be suppressed — check your Focus/DND settings and add ntfy as an exception if needed.
